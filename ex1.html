Aim

To synthesize accurate solid silica nanoparticles required for making the Biosensor container.

Background

Our project uses Hollow containers made up of mesoporous silica nanoparticles. The first step for preparing the box requires us to make solid silica particles, creating mesoporous silica layer over them and then etching the solid nanoparticles out. We need to create perfectly sized solid silica nanoparticles as this will then become the hollow part of our box and the volume of this part affects the diffusion rate of the sensing particles out of the box in the presence of the corresponding material.

Methods and Materials

  • In our first experiment we created our solid silica nanoparticles of the specific size needed (around 150 nm) using the standard Stober's process.
  • We tried ultra-sonication for different times and selected the best conditions out of all which would lead to minimal agglomeration of the particles.

Preparation of solid silica nanoparticles

  • 35.7 mL of absolute ethanol was mixed with 5 mL water and 0.8 mL of ammonia and stirred for 5–10 minutes at room temperature in falcon tube.
  • Then 1 mL of TEOS was added and the mixture was allowed to react in different conditions to standardize the protocol. The different conditions were:
    1. Stirred at room temperature for 1 h.
    2. Non-stirred at room temperature for 1 h.
    3. Stirred at room temperature for 30 min.
    4. Non-stirred at room temperature for 30 min.
  • Afterward, dSiO2 nanoparticles was washed with water and ethanol and centrifuged at 12000 rpm for 10 minutes.
  • The particles were then suspended in 10 mL of water (1X solution).

Ultra-centrifugation of the solid silica nanoparticles

  • We created 10X diluted solution for 10 different time conditions - 20 sec, 40 sec, 60 sec, 120 sec, 200 sec, 300 sec.
  • We then conducted sonication for respective time frames and four different conditions.

Aim

To coat mesoporous silica layer on the top of our already formed solid silica nanoparticles:

  • optimize the reaction conditions to get the best product
  • and determine the size of the overall nanoparticle

Background

Methods and Materials

Preparation of Mesoporous layer over the already prepared solid silica nanoparticles. We will use Triethanol amine (TEA) as ______.

  • Mix 2g of CTAB (cationic surfactant) and 20 mg of TEA in 20 ml of mili Q water.
  • Stir the mixture at room temperature for 1 hour.
  • Add 7.5 ml of 1X solid silica nanoparticle (SiO2) solution to the above mixture and stir the mixture at room temperature for 1 hour again.
  • Now, add 0.15 ml of Tetra Ethyl Orthosilicate (TEOS) to the mixture.
  • This mixture is stirred in two different conditions to standardize the protocol.
    1. 80 degree and 1 hour.
    2. 25 degree and 20 hour.

We found that 25 degree and 20 hours reaction condition gave us the best results. Following are the TEM images showing the prepared SiO2@MSN/CTAB (solid silica nanoparticles with mesoporous layer and CTAB in the pores).
